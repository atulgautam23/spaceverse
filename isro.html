water found on moon
india did a great job

India's Chandrayaan-1 mission in 2008 made a historic discovery of water on the Moon. Using NASA’s Moon Mineralogy Mapper, it detected water molecules in the lunar soil, especially near the Moon's poles. This finding suggested water exists in trace amounts across various regions, which could be used in future missions for drinking, agriculture, and creating fuel. This discovery has since driven more global missions to study lunar water, particularly in the Moon’s polar regions, opening up possibilities for future lunar bases.
